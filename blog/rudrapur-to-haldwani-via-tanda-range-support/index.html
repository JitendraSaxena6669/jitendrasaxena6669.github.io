Route: Rudrapur → Haldwani via Tanda Range

Directions

  1. Start from Rudrapur and head north-east on the local road toward the Tanda Range foothills.
  2. Begin climbing gently; enjoy panoramic views of the hills and occasional forested stretches.
  3. Reach Fauji Matkota Chaurah — a historic junction / memorial point.
  4. Continue upward and cross a vantage point with views toward Haldwani valley.
  5. Drive past the Ashok Leyland facility on the hillside road.
  6. Descend into Haldwani town via hill-side winding roads, reaching the city outskirts.

History & Importance: Fauji Matkota Chaurah

Fauji Matkota Chaurah holds significant historical value in this region. Originally established as a military rest point (“Fauji”) for soldiers during earlier decades, it has become a memorial junction where local lore remembers the service of armed personnel. “Matkota” is believed to refer to a local landmark or small hillock that once hosted water pots (“matkas”) for travelers and soldiers, making this chaurah (crossing) a vital pit-stop.

Over time, the place evolved into a community hub. Families from nearby hill villages visit on special occasions; the chaurah offers sweeping views of the lower hills and the valley ahead toward Haldwani. For travelers, it is both a cultural landmark and a place to pause, reflect, and absorb the natural beauty.

Industrial Landmark: Ashok Leyland on the Hill Road

As you drive along the Tanda Range, you’ll notice a facility of Ashok Leyland — a major commercial vehicle manufacturer. This presence is more than just industrial; it reflects how the region links rural hill terrain with heavy transport logistics. The facility supports local employment and contributes to the industrial ecosystem of Uttarakhand.

Safety, Etiquette & Travel Tips

Respect the hill communities: greet the locals, ask before photographing, and stay on marked roads. As you climb, drive carefully — hairpin bends, narrow stretches, and patches without guardrails are common.

Emergency & Practical Advice

Carry a first-aid kit, a torch or flashlight, extra water, and a power bank. In hill zones, mobile signals may drop; download offline maps beforehand.
